The Mission to Mars

NASA’s Mars Exploration Program is a high-stakes, science-driven initiative designed to unravel the mysteries of the Red Planet. By deploying sophisticated robotic explorers, we aim to characterize Mars as a dynamic system—investigating its complex geology, shifting climate cycles, and the fundamental question of whether life ever existed beyond Earth.

Through the lens of Curiosity and Perseverance, we are not just observing a distant world; we are preparing for the next giant leap in human exploration. From analyzing ancient riverbeds to testing oxygen production in the Martian atmosphere, every sample collected and every image transmitted brings us one step closer to understanding our place in the solar system.

Active Rover Missions

Perseverance Mission Update

Launched: July 2020 | Landed: Feb 2021

Perseverance is searching for signs of ancient microbial life. In the summer of 2024, it investigated a complex rock at "Cheyava Falls" showing signs of past water and organic material.

In September 2025, the journal Nature published validated results confirming the "Sapphire Canyon" sample contains potential biosignatures.

Source: NASA News Release

Curiosity Mission Update

Launched: Nov 2011 | Landed: Aug 2012

Recent studies suggest that the abundance of organic compounds found in Gale Crater could not be fully accounted for by non-biological sources alone. These findings may be fragments of ancient fatty acids.
